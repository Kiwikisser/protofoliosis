To Experience a Story

As our first project in the 2nd year of my Bachelor's degree, I were given the task to turn any real event into an interactive story. I personally enjoy stories or achievements that inspire awe, in which case I thought to turn a certain Elon Musk's SpaceX adventure into a more hands-on experience.

A Story Starts at the Storyboard

Before I got to programming, I needed to plan out the story I wished to tell. And just like any other story relying on visual material, I took a pencil and paper and started storyboarding.

A Story is Experienced Through Interaction

Having made the general trend of the story, I could move on to designing moments of interactions and their details. I did this with paper prototyping, designing it mobile first. Part of the focus of this project was for the story to be experienced through mobile devies as well as desktop. The supporting argument for this is that it's always easier to give existing elements more space on the screen, opposed to giving elements less space. Therefore the paper viewports were in shapes of phones and interactive elements as post-its.

A Story is Prototyped

The next step from the lo-fi paper prototype designs is its realisation to hi-fi examples. I chose to use the tool Figma to design my interactive story website. Figma allows to easily design website layouts, contents and interactions. It also allows for easy sharing of prototypes, which makes it very easy to test it with the target audience and collect valuable feedback. To this day I love Figma and use it, not only for website or apps, but to design anything in my mind (even a Dungeons & Dragons campaign!)

A Story is Refined

The iterative process of testing prototypes, collecting feedback and realising necessary changes, has been at the center of most projects in my Bachelor's. This was the second project where this process, taken from the Design Thinking methology, got put into practice. And extensively so. When my designs were not adequate, or failed to impress, I was not afraid to throw it away and start over. Improvement starts at realising points of failure. This took place at every step of the process; Storyboarding, Paper Prototyping, hi-fi Prototyping, and the final product.

A Story is Told With Technology

The most challenging and fun part of this project was letting my creativity loose on all the ways of interaction. Designing an interaction was one thing, but subsequently realising the designs within the limits of plain HTML, (S)CSS and JS, was a whole new beast.

A Story is Remembered

The year preceeding this project contained some basic classes in website programming, where simple webshops or imaginary restaurant websites were made. So jumping into this adventure, my creativity finally got a worthy challenge. I could not have asked a better way to kickstart our 2nd year, and wish to thank my teachers, Jeroen and Joel, for sharing their priceless knowledge and wisdom. In the end I received the third highest grade (8.3/10) in my class of approximately 50 students. It was mainly attributed to the various forms of interaction I managed to incorporate.
